To Inquire

The way the work is done

I do not approach ceramics as an artist creating an object, but as a practitioner in service of the present moment.

Each piece is a one-in-a-lifetime offering shaped through a deep inner practice of presence and devotion.

Before entering the studio, I follow a personal ceremonial process to open the working space and devote the work to the Great Spirit and to all the elements involved.

I pray for the work to be clear, for the present moment to guide me, and for those who will one day hold and use this piece. I welcome whatever will arise in the process, allowing the present moment to unfold through the hands. No personal intention, narrative, or emotional imprint is placed into the work. The ceramic must remain clean, open, and free to serve whoever it reaches.

On each piece

I do not create "collections" or serial pieces. Every ceramic vessel is born from the particular moment in which it was shaped through hands guided by trust in the Dao of things.

Before choosing

Before choosing a piece, please ask yourself if you feel aligned with this way of working.

This is not work driven by trends, marketing, or self-expression. It is a devotional practice — an offering to life itself.

If this approach resonates with you, the piece you are considering is now ready to serve.

This is not an online shop.
To inquire about a piece, reach out directly.
